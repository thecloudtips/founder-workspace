## Why It Works

The wordplay in the opening line works well: Suggesting that the definition of the word wealth is actually its opposite (expensive) is a bit of a mind-bender. This gets people to stop and lean in. Both of the opening lines are short and mobile friendly and play with people's brains. They only want to read more.

## Template

[Industry]'s definition of [success metric] is expensive.
It'll cost you everything.
The price of being 100% focused on "[common industry status symbol]" is:
- [Important cost 1]
- [Important cost 2]
- [Important cost 3]
- [Important cost 4]
The most [successful] [professionals] I know?
- [Counter-intuitive trait 1]
- [Counter-intuitive trait 2]
- [Counter-intuitive trait 3]
Their real assets stack up like this:
1. [True asset 1]
2. [True asset 2]
3. [True asset 3]
4. [True asset 4]
Always chasing the appearance of [success metric] keeps you from building *actual* [success metric].
True [success] is [wisdom statement about contentment/authenticity].

## Example

Marketing's definition of success is expensive.
It'll cost you everything.
The price of being 100% focused on "ranking #1" is:
- Your content quality
- Your audience trust
- Your brand authenticity
- Your sustainable growth
The most successful SEOs I know?
- Focus on user intent first
- Build content for humans
- Ignore "quick win" tactics
Their real assets stack up like this:
1. Evergreen content
2. Authentic backlinks
3. Engaged audience
4. Strategic patience
Always chasing the appearance of traffic keeps you from building *actual* authority.
True SEO success is serving your audience so well they can't help but share.
