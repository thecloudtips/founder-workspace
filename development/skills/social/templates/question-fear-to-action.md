## Why It Works

This mostly works for two reasons: it's decently mobile-optimized (61% of LinkedIn users on mobile), and it uses pain-agitate to really make people "feel" it deeply when reading it. Examples include: "Your brilliant ideas? Unrealized." / "Your potential? Untapped." / "When you hesitate, opportunities vanish."

## Template

[Obstacle] stops many [aspirations].
People ask: "[Negative question]?"
Better question: "[Positive question]?"
This shift is powerful.
It [positive outcome].
Remember: [Subject] can [negative effect] or [positive effect].
Struggling with [challenge]?
You're not alone.
Many [group] face this.
While you wait:
• [Missed opportunity 1]
• [Missed opportunity 2]
It's time to act:
1. [Action step 1]
2. [Action step 2]

## Example

Here's an example using the template, written from the perspective of an SAT Tutor:

Test anxiety stops many college dreams.
People ask: "What if I fail the SAT?"
Better question: "What if I ace the SAT?"
This shift is powerful.
It boosts confidence and focus.
Remember: Stress can paralyze or motivate you.
Struggling with SAT prep?
You're not alone.
Many high schoolers face this.
While you procrastinate:
- Dream schools become out of reach
- Scholarship opportunities slip away
It's time to act:
1. Set a study schedule
2. Take practice tests regularly
3. Hire an SAT coach to help you prep
