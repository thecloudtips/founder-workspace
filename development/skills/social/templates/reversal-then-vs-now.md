## Why It Works

It works because of the juxtaposition between two years that are a decade apart. If you can hook people with the bullets in the "old way," their natural curiosity will make them click "…more" to see the new way. By being blunt about the differences, you get people to lean in and engage.

## Template

[Year in the past]:
- [Common practice 1]
- [Common practice 2]
- [Common practice 3]
- [Outcome 1]
[Current or future year]:
- [New approach 1]
- [New approach 2]
- [New approach 3]
- [Improved outcome]
[One-sentence summary of the main point or advice]
[Specific action or place to start]
Use my [resource type]: [Link]
[Engagement question related to the topic]
P.S. [Optional: Address a potential objection or add a clarifying note]

## Example

2015:
- Climb the corporate ladder
- Follow traditional leadership models
- Focus solely on business metrics
- Struggle with work-life balance
2025:
- Create your own leadership path
- Embrace emotional intelligence
- Prioritize team well-being and business results
- Achieve sustainable success and personal fulfillment
The most effective leaders don't just manage teams; they inspire growth, foster innovation, and create positive change.
Start your leadership evolution today. Reflect on your current leadership style and identify areas for growth.
Use my Executive Leadership Assessment Tool: [Insert link here]
What's one leadership skill you believe will be crucial in the next decade?
P.S. Remember, great leadership is a journey, not a destination. Continuous learning is key.
